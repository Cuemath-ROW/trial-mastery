## Hypothesis Verdicts

### H1 — Attribution Gap ✅ CONFIRMED (with nuance)
*Teachers can't connect their behavior to trial outcomes — they attribute to the parent, not to what they did.*

**Signal across 10 teachers:**

- **T01** (0 conversions): "I feel the fee structure is a barrier." But when probed: *"Do parents bring up pricing with you in the trial?"* — Answer: **"No one had discussed with me about this. This is my assumption."** She attributes 0 conversions to something that didn't come up in a single trial.

- **T04** (9-year veteran): "In my trials, parents are satisfied and even check days and timings. But when the AC talks about pricing, they drop." Cannot describe a specific in-trial behavior that differentiated converting vs. non-converting trials. *"I don't understand what exactly went wrong there."*

- **T07** (8-year veteran): Noted one non-conversion where parent was "talking in Hindi only." When asked about what went differently: *"Nothing wrong was done from my end — it was just the parent intent."* Also observed parents jumping to fees mid-session but couldn't name what he would have done to redirect them.

- **T02** (high conversion): **OPPOSITE pattern.** Can narrate specific in-trial behaviors: *"I stopped the parent. I asked them to sit back and observe. I told the student: it's okay to make mistakes, I'm here."* When a trial failed: *"When parents step in, the child starts losing confidence. So I had to stop it."* Feedback loop intact.

- **T05** (10/10 conversion): Internal attribution — profiles the parent at the start ("ask them what exactly they are looking for") and explicitly pitches to that goal. Knows what she did. But: has stopped preparing because she already converts well — her attribution loop maintains performance but does not drive improvement.

- **T10** (very high conversion): Builds parent persona at start, pitches accordingly. *"I pitch everything as per their goal."* Can articulate what she did. **But when a trial didn't convert**: *"Parents were just smiling. Neither the parent nor the kid was serious. They came because it was free."* External attribution returns even for high-converters when trials fail.

**Pattern**: Attribution gap is real but **clusters by situation, not purely by teacher**. Low converters (T01, T04, T07) default to external attribution for ALL outcomes. High converters (T02, T05, T10) have internal attribution for successes but slip into external attribution when trials fail. The gap is structural — there is no mechanism in the system that gives a teacher a behavioral mirror after a trial. The "fee structure" defence (T01, T04) and "parent intent" defence (T07, T10) are thought-terminating explanations that short-circuit the learning loop.

### H2 — Preparation Vacuum ✅ CONFIRMED VERY STRONGLY
*Teachers do zero content preparation in the 30–60 minutes before a trial.*

| Teacher | Pre-trial preparation | Pull-to-material? |
|---------|----------------------|-----------------|
| T01 | Reviews trial sheets + reads WhatsApp summary | No curriculum content |
| T02 | "No, not actually." | No |
| T03 | "It goes naturally." | No |
| T04 | Checks sheets + parent requirements; keeps manual register | Closest — but no official resource |
| T05 | "Now I have taken so many — I don't prepare for anything." | No |
| T06 | No prep; didn't know trial's curriculum in advance (platform gap) | No |
| T07 | Checks platform worksheet layout once — not before each trial | No |
| T08 | Has training PDF; goes back "sometimes if extra exam comes up" | Closest — PDF exists, rarely used pre-trial |
| T09 | "I prepare reading material about the parent. If I am lucky, I get it." | No curriculum prep |
| T10 | Initially checked 3-level sheets. Now: "I'm pro in that. I don't see sheets." | No |

**9 of 10 teachers do zero curriculum or behavioral preparation immediately before a trial.** T08 is the marginal exception — she retrieves a PDF only when a specific unfamiliar exam comes up.

**Structural root**: There is no material designed for use 30 minutes before a trial. The module exists but is an onboarding resource, not a pre-trial reference. Critical quotes:

- T01: *"We only get 'APAC region.' I'd want to know if it's Singapore so I can prepare."*
- T07: *"Off late I am not receiving [AC briefings] on the student. Maybe I didn't receive — I didn't check."* AC pre-briefs that used to exist have degraded.
- T09: *"On the other side also they are coming with their eyes closed. We are also going with our eyes closed."*
- T04: Keeps his OWN MANUAL REGISTER of region-specific things he's learned — no official reference exists.

The T09 quote is the most crystalline statement of the mutual preparation vacuum: **both parties arrive at the trial blind.** Parents don't know what to expect from Cuemath. Teachers don't have the specific context to guide parents into confidence. The teacher is the only party we can fix.

---

### H3 — Identity Threat ❌ NOT CONFIRMED
*Experienced teachers disengage from training as an identity threat.*

None of the 10 teachers showed defensiveness about training. The opposite was true:

- T02: *"At WhiteHat, every class was monitored. We got feedback pinpointed at specific timestamps. That improved me enormously. Here, once in a while."* — active frustration at absence of training
- T04: Said "obviously yes" to using a training module if one existed
- T07: Explicitly asked for a brief, pointer-style training card: *"It should be shorter — highlighter. The bottom things needed for the trial."*
- T09: *"Provide them training very frequently so that it can be done."*
- T10: *"Definitely [feedback on non-converts would help]. That kind of thing I learned from WhiteHat only."*

The closest signal to H3 was T05 and T10: high converters who said they don't prepare anymore because they already convert well. This is not identity threat — it is **complacency of success**. You stop improving when there is no feedback loop showing what's broken.

**Implication**: The identity-frame intro (T1-A) was solving a problem that doesn't exist in this population. The design energy is better spent on delivery mechanism (when + how content reaches teachers) than on psychological framing.

---

### H4 — Implementation Intentions Gap ✅ CONFIRMED
*Teachers go into trials with no explicit behavioral intention set.*

| Teacher | Pre-trial intention | Type |
|---------|--------------------|----|
| T01 | "I will observe the requirements" | Reactive/passive |
| T02 | "I love interacting with children" | Motivational, not behavioral |
| T03 | "It goes naturally" | None |
| T04 | "I profile the parent before starting" | Closest — listening, not if-then |
| T05 | "I ask what they're looking for, then pitch to that" | Responsive, not pre-committed |
| T06 | No stated intention | None |
| T07 | "I just go to the platform and check the worksheets" | Logistical, not behavioral |
| T08 | No stated intention — reads parent type in first 2-3 minutes | Responsive, not pre-committed |
| T09 | "First: rapport with student. Second: parent confidence." | **Closest to if-then** — explicit priorities |
| T10 | Starts with "tell me about yourself and your child" | Framework entry, not if-then |

**0 of 10 teachers set an explicit behavioral if-then commitment** before a trial. T09 is closest — has explicit priorities she executes deliberately. T05 and T10 have a structured entry habit ("ask what they're looking for / about themselves") but this is a general protocol, not a trial-specific if-then commitment.

**Delivery timing note**: The commitment card (T1-C) only works if presented at the moment of pre-trial use, not embedded in a module completed weeks earlier.

## Cross-Cutting Patterns (Beyond the 4 Hypotheses)

### Pattern A — TCScore: Zero Awareness, Universal Motivation Gap

| Teacher | TCScore awareness | Motivation to convert more |
|---------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| T01 | **None** — "I don't have any idea" | High — frustrated at 0 conversions |
| T02 | Not explicitly probed | High — knows her conversion rate matters |
| T04 | Not probed | Low — full capacity |
| T05 | Not probed | Moderate — has 2 open slots |
| T07 | **None** (implied) | Low — reduced slots by choice |
| T08 | Not aware before call; Jishan explained it | High — income-driven |
| T09 | **None** (implied) | High — wants more students |
| T10 | **None** (implied) | Moderate — full schedule currently |

The mechanism is completely invisible to teachers. T01 is in a starvation loop she cannot diagnose: 0 conversions → fewer trials → fewer chances to improve → 0 conversions. T08 received the TCScore explanation during the call and immediately grasped it: *"So if I don't convert, my score gets down and I get fewer trials."* — confirming the mechanism is intuitive once explained.

---

### Pattern B — WhiteHat is the Benchmark They Compare Cuemath To

4 of 10 teachers came from WhiteHat Junior or BYJUs/FutureSchool (T02, T08, T09 background, T10). WhiteHat's training infrastructure is consistently described as superior — not in content knowledge, but in feedback:

- **T02**: *"At WhiteHat, every class was monitored. We got feedback pinpointed at specific timestamps. That improved me enormously. Here, once in a while."*
- **T08**: *"At WhiteHat, we used to get 2-3 trials a day. They made sure you were fully prepared — nothing was unknown."*
- **T10**: *"That kind of a thing [feedback on non-converts], I learned from WhiteHat only. They train you to answer each and every question."*
- **T09**: *"Show them a proper trial. Blur the teacher, blur the parent. The new teacher will at least know what to anticipate. Reading matter doesn't help."*

**Key distinction**: WhiteHat was a **performance feedback system** (recorded trials + timestamped review). The Trial Mastery module is a **knowledge delivery system**. These solve different problems. The module closes the knowledge gap. The WhiteHat model closed the execution gap. Both are needed. Two teachers independently recommended the same fix: video snippets of actual trials.

---

### Pattern C — Parents Jump to Fees Before the Learning Plan Lands

- **T07**: *"At the end once we share these levels and explain to them they are quickly jumping to only the fees. They are not actively listening to the roadmap of what the teacher has to say."*
- **T01**: Attributes non-conversion to fees without parents ever raising it — fee obsession happening in the teacher's head.
- **T10**: Had a parent who opened with *"this is a free class"* — no purchase intent — but T10 tried anyway and converted.

High converters (T05, T09, T10) demonstrate value *during* the session — not at the end. Low converters let the trial drift without establishing value first, leaving the parent's first decision point as the pricing page.

**Gap**: The module (Part E — closing sequence) does not currently teach *when* in the trial sequence the value proposition must be made, only *what* to say about value. Sequencing knowledge is missing.

---

### Pattern D — New Teachers Have a Training-Quality Lottery Problem

- **T10** (bad trainer "Pauthya"): *"The teacher was not ready to tell me anything. She haven't told me about the puzzle thing. I had zero idea. I called my friend who also teaches at QMath."* — Found platform basics through peer network, not Cuemath.
- **T08** (had training PDF): Uses it, but inconsistently. Training quality appears better.

T10 explicitly flagged: her bad trainer experience covers only the first 2 sessions. *"Rest everything was really nice — the last three classes were great."* The inconsistency is in trainer quality, not material quality.

**Implication**: The Trial Mastery module has an unintended high-value secondary purpose — a standardised knowledge base that removes dependency on trainer quality. A teacher who got a bad trainer can self-serve the content. This is a stronger case for the module than any of the original 4 hypotheses.

---

### Pattern E — Curriculum/Geography Context is the #1 Stated Need (8 of 10 Teachers)

8 of 10 teachers raised — unprompted — a need for better curriculum/geography context before trials:

- T01: *"If the confirmation tells me which country, I could research."*
- T02: *"When I switched from US to UK, I had no idea year levels were offset by one. There was no module."*
- T04: *"There is no ready-recognised guide I can go back to. I keep a manual register."*
- T06: Trial had curriculum confusion — UK curriculum unavailable; had to choose wrong board in front of parent.
- T07: Asked Jishan about curriculum structure (CPA) during the interview — revealing a gap in understanding the platform's own logic.
- T08: *"If someone says NAPLAN, you should be aware what is covered — which grades, when the deadline. If you're confident, they get very confident."*
- T09: *"There is something called 7 plus. Many teachers don't know about it. Only when you start teaching there do you come to know."*
- T10: *"Nobody told us anything about the curriculum [for Australia]. Like you define it — it is necessary."*

The module **already contains this content** (Part A for APAC and EUK). The problem is **delivery timing and discoverability** — not content. Teachers completed it at onboarding weeks ago, cannot find it again, and have no mechanism to retrieve it at the point of need.

---

### Pattern F — Platform Experience Is Undermining the Trial (Out of Module Scope)

Multiple teachers raised platform-level issues that directly reduce conversion — outside the module but critical to flag:

- **T06**: UK curriculum option missing from platform during trial → forced to choose wrong board → confidence destroyed in front of parent
- **T09**: Student cannot write on platform → teacher writes everything → parent worries about paid class experience
- **T09**: Trial session used to cut off at 55 minutes mid-parent-conversation (bug — may be resolved)
- **T10**: Victorian curriculum shows one year-level behind what the student is in → confusion during trial
- **T10**: Enrichment programs — *"nobody told us how to use them — nobody cleared that"*
- **T10**: Puzzles shown in trial unavailable in regular classes → student asks; teacher can't deliver
- **T07**: AC pre-briefs (student info) have degraded — teachers not receiving them regularly

These are conversion problems the module cannot fix. They require platform and ops intervention.

## The Single Most Important Finding

> *"On the other side also they are coming with their eyes closed. We are also going with our eyes closed."*
> — T09, 5+ year EUK teacher

**Both parties arrive at the trial blind.** Parents don't know what to expect from Cuemath. Teachers don't have the specific context to guide parents into confidence. The fix is not more module content — it is a mechanism that puts the right content in front of the teacher **30 minutes before their specific trial**.

## Proposed Changes — Priority Order

### P1 — Pre-Trial Brief Card (T1-D) → **Build this first**
*Status: NOT built. Deferred in original redesign plan.*

A 5–8 line card, specific to the student's geography/curriculum, on the module homepage — accessible any time, no gate.

**Content**: Region + Key Stage/year-level primer + 2-3 parent archetypes most likely for that region + competitive exam dates + most common objection. 60 seconds to read.

**Why P1**: Directly solves the most confirmed gap (H2). 8 of 10 teachers asked for it. T04 already maintains a manual version. The content is in the module — retrieval timing is missing.

**Design note**: Prominent homepage button: "Pre-Trial Brief →" with region dropdown. No quiz, no submission. Optional: AC team sends WhatsApp link 1 hour before trial slot.

---

### P2 — TCScore Bridge (T1-B) → **Deploy as first slide**
*Status: Built, not deployed.*

A single static slide before any content: "Your conversion rate determines how many trials you get. Teachers who convert more get first access."

**Why P2**: Zero awareness across 10 teachers. T01 is in an undiagnosed starvation loop. T08 grasped the mechanism immediately when explained. This is already built, requires zero engineering, and is the highest motivational ROI per minute of effort.

**Caution**: Confirm the mechanism is accurate before deploying. If TCScore is not actively driving allocation today, this slide is a credibility risk.

---

### P3 — Attribution Mirror in Scenario Challenges (T1-F) → **Minor copy edit**
*Status: Scenarios built; attribution framing missing.*

After each scenario answer is confirmed, add 2 lines:
> *"When you did X, the parent's trust went up because [reason]. This is the moment in most trials where the conversion decision gets made — not at the pricing page."*

Critical for the fee-objection scenario specifically. T01 and T04 need to see explicitly that the fee defence is a post-hoc rationalisation for an unidentified in-trial failure.

---

### P4 — Post-Trial Reflection Card → **New lightweight addition**
*Status: Not in the module.*

A 3-question private reflection card on the module homepage: *"What went well? What would you do differently? What did the parent seem most concerned about?"*

No backend, no submission. Value is in the act of reflection. Optional: WhatsApp message 2 hours after trial slot: "How did your trial go? [Quick reflection link]"

T09 and T10 both explicitly asked for trial feedback after non-conversions. This is the lightest feasible version.

---

### P5 — Sequencing Guidance in Part E → **Content addition**
*Status: Gap not in original design.*

T07's pattern — parents jumping to fees before the learning plan lands — is a sequencing problem. High converters demonstrate value *during* the session; low converters let it drift. Part E (closing sequence) currently covers what to say about value, not *when* in the trial sequence value must be demonstrated.

Add: Explicit instruction on the moment in the trial where the parent's commitment forms, and what must already be visible to the parent by that moment.

---

### P6 — Video Trial Snippets → **Highest-leverage medium-term build**
*Status: Not planned.*

T09 and T10 recommended this independently and unprompted.

T09: *"You can blur the teacher, blur the parent. The new teacher will at least have an idea of the flow. Reading, you will read and read — but actually seeing, it is easy to sit in the mind."*

T10: *"What White Hat did was make you ready to answer each and every question. That kind of thing I learned from WhiteHat only. They trained you."*

**What**: 3–5 minute edited clips of top-performing trials. One per region. Embedded in the module's intro section. Source from existing AC-recorded sessions.

**Not Q2** — requires sourcing, consent, and production. Q3 initiative. But decide now.

---

### P7 — Keep T1-A Identity Frame Intro
*Status: Built. No further investment.*

Identity threat is not the primary blocker for this population. Keep the intro wizard as-is. Do not deprioritise, but do not invest further.

## Caveats

- **Interviewer influence**: Jishan conducted as Jishan, not blind. Social desirability likely suppressed training criticism and amplified product feedback. Self-reported conversion rates (T05's "10/10") should be discounted — cross-reference with actual AC data.
- **India-based teachers**: All 10 are India-based taking international trials. The pre-trial context gap (H2) may be smaller for locally-based AU/UK teachers — but this is Cuemath's model.
- **Platform bugs distort data**: T06's trial was ruined by a platform bug. T09's experience includes older session cutoffs. These lower apparent teacher performance in ways the module cannot fix.
- **Selection bias**: ACs referred teachers they knew well. The lowest-performing, most disengaged teachers may not be represented.
