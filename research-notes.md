## 1. Australian schooling structure

- Schools run **Foundation/Kindergarten → Year 12**. Compulsory ~age 6 through 15–17 (state-dependent).
- Ages at entry: kids start preliminary year between **4.5–5.5 years** of age.
- **Terminology varies by state**: NSW/ACT/QLD/SA/WA/NT = "Kindergarten" (Year K); Victoria = "Prep"; SA historically = "Reception"; Tasmania compulsory from age 5.
- **Primary school**: F/K → Year 6 (some states F → Year 7 historically). **Secondary**: Year 7 → Year 12.
- School year runs **February → December**, 4 terms.
- Enrolment split: ~64% government · 19.7% Catholic · 16.3% independent/private.
- Senior certificates by state: NSW = **HSC**, VIC = **VCE**, WA = **WACE**, QLD = **QCE**, SA = **SACE**, TAS = TCE, ACT = ACT Senior Secondary Certificate.
- **Source**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Education_in_Australia

## 2. NAPLAN (National Assessment Program — Literacy and Numeracy)

- Years tested: **3, 5, 7, 9**.
- Timing: **March each year** (moved from May in 2023).
- Tests **4 domains**: Reading, Writing, Conventions of Language (spelling/grammar/punctuation), **Numeracy**.
- Reported in **10 performance bands**. National Minimum Standard: Band 2 for Year 3 → Band 6 for Year 9.
- Online delivery is default.
- Parents often start prep **2–3 months ahead**. Test-prep industry is significant.
- **Sources**: https://nap.edu.au/naplan · https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NAPLAN

## 3. NSW Selective High Schools Test

- Entry to Year 7 at NSW government selective high schools.
- Students sit it in **Year 6**.
- **~15,000 students** apply for **~3,600 places**.
- Age requirement: 11y5m to 13y at start of Year 7.
- **4 components** (Cambridge-style, new from 2021):
  - Thinking Skills: 40 Qs / 40 min
  - **Mathematical Reasoning: 35 Qs / 40 min**
  - Reading Skills: 38 Qs / 45 min
  - Writing: 30 min
- Top schools: **James Ruse, North Sydney Boys/Girls, Sydney Boys/Girls, Baulkham Hills, Hornsby Girls, Normanhurst Boys**.
- Results late August.
- **Source**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selective_school_(New_South_Wales)

## 4. NSW Opportunity Classes (OC)

- Entry to Year 5 OC classes in NSW government primary schools. Advanced placement for "gifted and talented" Year 5–6 students.
- Students sit the OC test in **Year 4**.
- Tests: Reading, **Mathematical Reasoning**, Thinking Skills.
- Highly competitive. Parents start prep as early as Year 3.
- Parents who mention OC are **very focused, time-sensitive, high-intent**.
- **Source**: NSW Department of Education site (URL redirected; verify with https://education.nsw.gov.au)

## 5. Victorian Selective Entry / SEAL

- Victoria has 4 selective entry high schools: **Melbourne High (boys), Mac.Robertson Girls' High, Suzanne Cory High, Nossal High**.
- Selection test held in the year before Year 9 entry.
- **SEAL** (Select Entry Accelerated Learning) programs run at various government schools for high-achievers Year 7+.
- Many Indian-Australian families in Melbourne target these.

## 6. Scholarship exams (private schools)

- Private schools in Melbourne/Sydney offer **academic scholarships** via:
  - **ACER** (Australian Council for Educational Research scholarship tests)
  - **Edutest**
  - **AAS** (Academic Assessment Services)
- Typically for Year 7 entry; students sit tests in **Year 5 or Year 6**.
- Cover maths, reading comprehension, writing, reasoning.
- Also national competitions: **ICAS, AMC (Australian Mathematics Competition), APSMO Maths Olympiad, SASMO**.

## 8. Learning science — 6 strategies (Learning Scientists)

1. **Spaced practice** — distribute learning over time rather than cramming. Improves retention.
2. **Retrieval practice** — actively recall, don't re-read. The testing effect.
3. **Elaboration** — connect new info to existing knowledge. Explain the why.
4. **Interleaving** — mix topic types during practice. Improves discrimination.
5. **Concrete examples** — use tangible specific instances to anchor abstract principles.
6. **Dual coding** — pair words with visuals. Leverages multiple memory systems.

**Source**: https://www.learningscientists.org (downloadable materials)

## 9. Additional learning principles

- **Leitner system** — physical card-box implementation of spaced repetition. Cards move forward when answered correctly, back when wrong. Typical intervals: 1 → 3 → 7 → 14 → 30 days.
- **Desirable difficulties (Bjork)** — moderate effort in learning = better retention than easy repetition.
- **Microlearning** — 5–10 min units beat hour-long lectures for retention.
- **Worked example → faded example → independent problem** — scaffold removal for mastery.
